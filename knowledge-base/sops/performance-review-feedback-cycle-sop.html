Review Cadence: Quarterly

Purpose

This SOP establishes Canopi's first formal performance review process: a quarterly, 360-style feedback cycle combining self-assessment, peer feedback, and manager review. This is a fresh process with no prior framework to build on — the goal for now is growth and feedback, not a formal rating or a tie to compensation or promotion decisions.

Scope

Covers the quarterly review cycle for all Canopi employees, from cycle kickoff through the delivered review conversation and documented summary. Does not currently determine promotion, compensation, or equity decisions — this is a feedback and growth process only, and can be revisited if the company wants to formally connect it to those decisions later.

Process Flow (Summary)

  • 1. Angela Tino kicks off the quarterly cycle and notifies all employees

  • 2. Each employee completes a self-assessment

  • 3. Peers (typically same-team members) submit feedback on each other

  • 4. The employee's manager compiles the self-assessment, peer feedback, and their own observations into a review

  • 5. Manager delivers the review in a 1:1 conversation with the employee

  • 6. Manager or Angela Tino documents summary notes for the record

  • 7. Cycle closes; next quarter's cycle begins on schedule

  • Detailed Steps
  • Step 1: Kick Off Cycle
  • Who: Angela Tino

How: At the start of each quarter, Angela notifies all employees that the review cycle is open and shares timelines/deadlines for self-assessments and peer feedback.

Output: Cycle officially open.

  • Step 2: Self-Assessment
  • Who: Employee

How: Each employee reflects on their own performance, wins, and growth areas over the past quarter and documents this in a self-assessment.

Output: Completed self-assessment.

Step 3: Peer Feedback

Who: Peers (typically same-team members, see Manager & Peer Group Reference)

How: Teammates submit feedback on the employee's collaboration, strengths, and areas to grow, based on the past quarter's working relationship.

Output: Peer feedback submitted.

Step 4: Compile Review

Who: Manager (see Manager & Peer Group Reference for who manages whom)

How: The manager reviews the self-assessment and peer feedback alongside their own observations, and compiles this into a single review.

Output: Compiled review ready to deliver.

  • Step 5: Deliver Review
  • Who: Manager → Employee

How: Manager delivers the review as a conversation (not just a written document), ideally as part of or alongside a regular 1:1.

Output: Employee has clear, direct feedback and space to respond.

  • Step 6: Document Summary
  • Who: Manager or Angela Tino

How: Key takeaways and any follow-up items from the review conversation are documented for the record (a natural fit for Meetings > One-on-One Agendas in the repository).

Output: Review cycle closed out and on file.

Exceptions and Edge Cases

Scenario What to Do
Employee has been in their role less than 6 weeks at cycle start Skip the formal review for that quarter; do a lightweight check-in instead. They join the full cycle the following quarter.
Reviewing a Team Head (Jagun Rimes or Dixon Lin) Bionka Randolph acts as manager and compiles/delivers the review. Peer feedback comes from the other Team Head and Angela Tino.
Reviewing Bionka Randolph (CEO/Founder) No formal manager exists above Bionka. For now, this is self-reflection plus informal peer input from Jagun, Dixon, and Angela — not a formal manager-led review. Revisit if a board or advisor is established.
Peer feedback across teams Default peer group is the employee's own team. Cross-team peer input is welcome but not required — confirm if you'd like this made mandatory as the team grows.
