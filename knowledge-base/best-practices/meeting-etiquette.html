Meeting Etiquette Published v1

Download original Word document

Prepared By: Angela Tino, Executive Operations Lead

Last Updated: July 22, 2026

Applies To: All Canopi meetings — leadership, team, 1:1, and cross-team

1. Purpose

This is a practical guide to running meetings at Canopi well — agendas, note-taking, and follow-through — so meeting time is actually useful and nothing important gets lost afterward.

2. Before the Meeting: Agendas

  • Every recurring meeting should have an agenda, shared far enough in advance that people can add to it or come prepared — ideally the day before, at minimum a few hours ahead.

  • An agenda item should be a specific topic or decision needed, not just “catch up” — if there's nothing concrete to discuss, consider cancelling or shortening the meeting.

  • If a meeting recurs weekly, start the agenda with a quick look at open action items from last time (see Section 4) before moving to new topics.

  • Ask: could this be a Slack message or async update instead of a meeting? If yes, skip the meeting.

3. During the Meeting

  • Start and end on time — both matter, especially across a distributed team juggling different schedules.

  • Stick to the agenda; if something unrelated but important comes up, note it and follow up separately rather than derailing the meeting.

  • One conversation at a time — avoid side threads that leave part of the group out of the discussion.

  • Designate a note-taker at the start (see Section 4 and the Meeting Types Reference below for defaults).

4. Note-Taking

Notes don't need to capture everything said — they need to capture what people will actually need later:

  • Key decisions made

  • Action items, each with a clear owner and, where possible, a due date

  • Anything that needs follow-up outside the meeting

Use the standard minutes template (Resources > Templates) for consistency, and file notes under Meetings > Meeting Minutes so they're easy to find later, filterable by team and date.

5. Action Items & Follow-Ups

  • Every action item needs an owner — “the team” isn't an owner.

  • Log action items in the master action item tracker (Meetings > Action Items) so they're visible beyond just the people in the room.

  • Start the next occurrence of a recurring meeting by checking outstanding action items from last time, rather than letting them quietly disappear.

  • If an action item is overdue, the owner should flag it proactively rather than waiting to be asked.

6. Meeting Types Quick Reference

Defaults for who owns the agenda and notes, by meeting type — adjust as needed for a specific meeting:

Meeting Type Agenda Owner Note-Taker Where Notes Live
Leadership (exec sync, planning) Bionka Randolph Angela Tino Meetings > Leadership Meetings
Team (standups, sprint, sync) Relevant Team Head Rotates or Team Head's call Meetings > Team Meetings
One-on-Ones Manager Optional — manager's call Meetings > One-on-One Agendas
Cross-team Whoever calls the meeting Whoever calls the meeting assigns one Meetings > Team Meetings

7. General Best Practices

  • Only invite people who actually need to be there — optional attendees can get the notes afterward instead.

  • Default to shorter meetings; it's easier to extend a meeting that's running productively than to fill unused time.

  • Cameras on is encouraged for team and leadership meetings where practical, since it helps a remote team stay connected — but isn't a strict requirement.

  • If a meeting keeps going over time or losing focus, that's a signal to revisit whether it needs a tighter agenda or a different format.
